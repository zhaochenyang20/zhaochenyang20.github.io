Engrave Your Name On The Hall Of Fame

The turmoil on campus in late November has forever changed me and the country behind me. Then, Academic Affairs issued a notice of open P / F, which undoubtedly dragged the times into a new wave of chaos. Regardless of what people may think, these chaotic times were just right for my academic and research aspirations.

The time, the place, the people, what else am I missing?

Objectively, it's true that they don't match her project as you. But you should think of it this way - It is too good of an opportunity for anyone to be a perfect match. If you are not an ideal match, you applied to it, so why can't others?

客观上，人家的确没你匹配。不过你该这么想——这个机会太好了，没有什么人完美匹配。既然你不是完美匹配也去申请了，那么别人为什么不能呢？

Thinking further, I also hope that this experience of breaking my defense due to competitive pressure motivates me to keep going. The fact that we will be competing fiercely for these opportunities today is a result of the extreme scarcity of resources. In America, it is far easier for undergraduates to get world-class research resources than at Tsinghua. I am determined to realize my ultimate ideal, break all unjust systems, and make a new world where everyone can make full use of their talents and abilities.

更进一步想，我也希望这次因为竞争压力而破防的经历激励我不断走下去。如今我们会为了一些研究机会而激烈竞争，本质上还是资源的极度稀缺。而在美国本土，本科生想要得到世界一流的研究资源远比清华容易。我只有坚定自我的初心，实现我的最终理想，破除一切不公正的制度，要让社会变成人尽其才物尽其用各得其乐的新世界，即使心在井隅，更要心向光明。

The Touch Of The City

Last night, the news that the lockdown in Guangzhou ended spread worldwide. When I questioned this as another inconsistent policy of the authorities, I found out that the information was actual.

A few weeks ago, the authorities said they would liberalize Shijiazhuang, which resulted in a farce. What a pity, Hebei people. What value is left but to be drained of the last blood by Beijing? Now that I've seen the news from Guangzhou, I suddenly realize that the era of chaos and turmoil seems to be over. Three years of epidemics have taught me that freedom can never be taken for granted. Guangzhou has been China's only port since the Qing Dynasty and has been a pioneer of reform until now. Shockingly, I was lamenting the turmoil of the times last week. I sent Anki back to Changsha sooner and prepared for the worst if the situation became more volatile. If that happened, I would immediately escape to Hong Kong and leave the mainland. However, things seem to have improved, and our people's struggle has come to fruition, making me happy. However, it took such a great sacrifice and cost for our people to gather the courage to fight against the authorities and force the government to accept political accountability. What a sadness of the mainland!

In conclusion, the three-year so-called natural disaster of the 21st century has finally ended. In the history books, some years later, we, the generation of students who spent our college career under the epidemic, will be just the generation who took some detours. But these three years have shaped my life. In fact, the detours lasted only a short time; at the beginning of the 22nd year, the authorities' zero covid policy was considered a detour. However, these three years, I never wanted to go through it again. Finally, I dedicate the following text from my Moments:

那是 20 年，是我高三记忆里最为艰苦的时日，然而那时候生活的烟火味至今让我动容。

Personal Statement

"The Limits of My Language Mean the Limits of My World."

by Ludwig Josef Johann Wittgenstein

昔者苍颉作书，而天雨粟、鬼夜哭。——《淮南子·本经训》

People Revered The Gods And, Therefore, The Written Words

Language and written words have had a sacred place in my heart since before I was exposed to computer science or even before I entered elementary school. I am of Qiang ethnicity and grew up in a minority area on the border of the Sichuan-Tibet Plateau when I was a little boy. At that time, I could feel the reverence of my homeland townies for the written word.

My grandmother used to tell me about the rules of the old days. People revered the gods and, therefore, the written words. Suppose you see a piece of paper with words on the roadside, whether it is in Tibetan or Chinese, you have to wipe your hands, pick it up carefully, put it into the cleanest pocket on your body, carry it home, and then burn it in front of the Buddha, and pray with devotion. If a child does not know what to do and steps on a piece of paper with written words on it, they will be reprimanded by the family's elders.

In such an atmosphere, I grew up with a reverence for language and written words, knowing that they represented holiness.

From the age of 6, I began to learn linguistics systematically. I studied Tibetan, Qiang, Chinese, and English. At the same time, I read a lot of language and literature, which made me fall in love with this subject and planted my ambition to become a writer.

When I entered university, I studied computer science, which seemed the opposite of my dream of becoming a writer. However, computer science and language and literature are consistent. I realized that many people before me in the field of computer science were also excellent writers—for example, Jun Wu, Paul Graham, and others. One of the people I admire most is Chip Huyen, a computer scientist at Stanford University.

I'm Chip Huyen, a writer, and computer scientist. I grew up chasing grasshoppers in a small rice-farming village in Vietnam.

I'm a co-founder of Claypot AI, a platform for real-time machine learning. Previously, I built machine learning tools at NVIDIA, Snorkel AI, Netflix, and Primer.

Very fortunately, I have a very similar life experience to her. I grew up chasing grasshoppers in a small potatoes-farming village in Sichuan-Tibet Plateau. In addition, I also insisted on documenting my college life in words. My personal blog has accumulated over 70,000 visitors. What's most impressive is that I am also obsessed with machine learning, especially natural language processing.
