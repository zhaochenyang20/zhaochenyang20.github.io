清华园日记·拾壹——乱世出英雄

发表于2022-11-01|更新于2022-12-01|写作清华园日记
|字数总计:968|阅读时长:5分钟|阅读量:

Readme

The turmoil on campus in late November has forever changed me and the country behind me. Then, Academic Affairs issued a notice of open P/F, which undoubtedly dragged the times into a new wave of chaos. Regardless of what people may think, these chaotic times were just right for my academic and research aspirations.

The time, the place, the people, what else am I missing?

Everything is ready. All I need is the east wind.

Calm and collected, give back a fatal blow.

November 30th

This morning when I woke up, Yu Kai asked me if Zijing II was closed because he saw some White Terrorists go in. I immediately questioned the floor manager whether our building was closed. The result is sealed ...

I agree to seal the building, but I strongly disapprove of the preemptive strike, and the whole process of message notification is very confusing.

To be funny, Yuchen’s PCR went positive last night. I realised that the current nucleic acid test is garbage. Ten people were tested together, and they just clicked on my tongue each time. That's how the fuck someone can still go positive. Then, once a batch of people is positive, all ten people’s dormitory buildings will be on lockdown. So, last night, Huazi tested positive for four tubes, sealing more than thirty dormitory buildings, half of all its students. Such a wide range of closures made the whole process chaotic and bizarre. Material distribution and so on are highly problematic. I wait till about two o'clock to eat lunch. Of course, the school sent snacks to appease the students, and eventually, someone was grateful. I'm convinced. What if you are sealed up one day, no snacks are sent, no food is dispensed, and you are left to starve?

Any trampling on my right to freedom, I will rise against.

After the building closure ended, I kicked the stairway door four times to express my rebellion against this shameful policy. In addition, several buildings tested positive today, and the positive cases are spreading. I'm impressed that this shameless government thinks this virus is defensible.

同一时间传来了互相矛盾的消息：上校在比利亚努埃瓦取得了胜利；在古阿卡马耶尔遭到了失败；被摩蒂龙部落的印第安人吃掉；死于沼泽地带的一个村庄；重新在乌鲁米特发动了起义。这时，自由党领袖正在跟政府举行关于容许自由党人进入国会的谈判，宣布他为冒险分子，不能代表他们的党。政府把他算做强盗，悬赏五千比索取他的首级。在十六次失败以后，奥雷连诺上校率领两千装备很好的印第安人，离开瓜希拉，进攻列奥阿察，惊惶失措的警备队逃出了这个城市。

News is that some cities in the south have chosen to liberalizeliberalise again. There is also news that Beijing is building square cabin hospitals on a large scale and that there will be concentration camps in every college. Considering the trampling of people's rights to freedom by the authorities' erratic policies, I will not make any statement. But when the financial collapse is complete, if those bastards do not leave the mainland, they will wait until the people push them to the guillotine, and their whole family will be exterminated.

I can only pray that the authorities will continue suffering for two more years and then completely collapse when I leave the mainland and make these bastards pay back what they’ve done to my people.

After the chaos, I sent Anki away, hoping we would be different again the next time I saw her.

Chaos

  1. I've seen the report that Catherine interviewed me for a few days ago. It's exciting that I could use this report and my self-pre to write the essay for my English class, and I may even be able to continue to write my PHS next year.
  2. By the way, Jiang Zemin passed away today. I deliberately went on Twitter to see that so many voices used to curse his early death were gone when he really died. Thirty years ago, it just so happened that Zhao Ziyang died, and then there was a massive upheaval in mourning for the old-time leader. Jiang Zemin passed away on November 16, only to release the obituary today. I can't help but feel that if the obituary had been released earlier and in time for the events in Xinjiang, the events of thirty years ago would have been repeated. Finally, when the news of Jiang Zemin's death came, I was dismayed by the overwhelming outpouring of condolences worldwide. I had heard countless times before that many dirty things had happened when he was in office, yet he was still so respected after his death. It's hard not to think that the bastards in power now have reached a point where the public can't stand it.
