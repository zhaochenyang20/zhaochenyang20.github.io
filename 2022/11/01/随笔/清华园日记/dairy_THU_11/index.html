People Revered The Gods And, Therefore, The Written Words

Language and written words have had a sacred place in my heart since before I was exposed to computer science or even before I entered elementary school. I am of Qiang ethnicity and grew up in a minority area on the border of the Sichuan-Tibet Plateau when I was a little boy. At that time, I could feel the reverence of my homeland townies for the written word.

My grandmother used to tell me about the rules of the old days. People revered the gods and, therefore, the written words. Suppose you see a piece of paper with words on the roadside, whether it is in Tibetan or Chinese, you have to wipe your hands, pick it up carefully, put it into the cleanest pocket on your body, carry it home, and then burn it in front of the Buddha, and pray with devotion. If a child does not know what to do and steps on a piece of paper with written words on it, they will be reprimanded by the family's elders.

In such an atmosphere, I grew up with a reverence for language and written words, knowing that they represented holiness.

From the age of 6, I began to learn linguistics systematically. I studied Tibetan, Qiang, Chinese, and English. At the same time, I read a lot of language and literature, which made me fall in love with this subject and planted my ambition to become a writer.

When I entered university, I studied computer science, which seemed the opposite of my dream of becoming a writer. However, computer science and language and literature are consistent. I realized that many people before me in the field of computer science were also excellent writersâ€”for example, Jun Wu, Paul Graham, and others. One of the people I admire most is Chip Huyen, a computer scientist at Stanford University.

I'm Chip Huyen, a writer, and computer scientist. I grew up chasing grasshoppers in a small rice-farming village in Vietnam.

I'm a co-founder of Claypot AI, a platform for real-time machine learning. Previously, I built machine learning tools at NVIDIA, Snorkel AI, Netflix, and Primer.

Very fortunately, I have a very similar life experience to her. I grew up chasing grasshoppers in a small potatoes-farming village in Sichuan-Tibet Plateau. In addition, I also insisted on documenting my college life in words. My personal blog has accumulated over 70,000 visitors. What's most impressive is that I am also obsessed with machine learning, especially natural language processing.
