Enhancing photorealism enhancement

Created2022-05-06|Updated2022-05-06|科研
|Word count:187|Reading time:1min|Post View:

Overview

The paper’s descriptional video is here. With 22 citations, the video got 400 w watchers.

Methods

  1. image enhancement network

  1. training methods
  1. LPIPS loss penalizes large structural differences between the input and output images.
  2. A perceptual discriminator evaluates the realism of output images.
  3. A specific sampling strategy for selecting real and synthetic image patches significantly reduces artifacts that are commonly observed in prior work.

Leveraging conventional rendering pipelines

Specifically, we extract G-buffers that provide information about geometric structure (surface normals, depth), materials (shader IDs, albedo, specular intensity, glossiness, transparency), and lighting (approximate irradiance and emission, sky, bloom)

We further augment this set with two
quantities we derive from the G-buffers. First, we reflect the view vector at the surface normal for each pixel to obtain a reflection vector. Second, we compute the dot product between the surface normal and the reflection vector.

Substantial enhancements can be achieved even if only a subset of the buffers is available.

We fuse the streams based on masks derived from the semantic segmentation maps. This way, the streams can map information from the G-buffers differently for certain types of objects.
