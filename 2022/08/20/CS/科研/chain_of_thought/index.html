Chain of Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models

Intro

  1. Generating intermediate results signifificantly improves accuracy when a

    desired input-output mapping involves multiple computational steps.

  2. Second, large language models offer the exciting prospect of in-context few-shot learning via prompting.

  3. explore the ability of large language models to perform few-shot prompting for reasoning tasks, given a prompt that consists of triples: <input, chain of thought, output>

Chain of thought

  1. First, chain of thought, in principle, allows models to decompose multi-step problems into intermediate steps, ==which means that additional computation can be allocated to problems that require more reasoning steps.==

  2. An interpretable window provide opportunities to debug

  3. math word problems, commonsense reasoning, and symbolic manipulation, and is applicable (in principle) to any task that humans can solve via language.

  4. ==Finally, chain of thought reasoning can be readily elicited in suffificiently large off-the-shelf language models simply by including examples of chain of thought sequences into the exemplars of few-shot prompting.==

Arithmetic Reasoning

  1. Standard prompting: The model gives the answer directly.
  2. We qualitatively found that models of smaller scale produced flfluent but illogical chains of thought, leading to lower performance than standard prompting.

Ablation Study

Whether thesame performance improvements can be conferred via other types of prompting? Three variations of chain of thought.

  1. Why chain of thought prompting might help is that it produces the

    mathematical equation to be evaluated.

    But the semantics of the questions in GSM8K are too challenging to directly translate into an equation without the natural language reasoning steps in chain of thought.

  2. Chain of thought allows the model to spend more computation on harder problems.

==But variable computation by itself is not the reason for the success of chain of thought prompting, and that there appears to be utility from expressing intermediate steps via natural language.==

  1. Allow the model to better access relevant knowledge acquired during pretraining.

    The sequential reasoning embodied in the chain of thought is useful for reasons beyond just activating knowledge.

Robustness

Sensitivity to exemplars is a key consideration of prompting approaches.

This result implies that successful use of chain of thought does not depend on a particular linguistic style.

independently-written chains of thought, different exemplars,

Chain of thought prompting for arithmetic reasoning is robust to different exemplar orders and varying numbers of exemplars.

Commonsence Reasoning

Applicable to a broad class of commonsense reasoning problems, which involve reasoning about physical and human interactions under the presumption of general background knowledge.

Symbolic Reasoning

==What is Symbolic Reasoning==

Not only enables language models to perform symbolic reasoning tasks that are challenging in the standard prompting setting, but also ==facilitates length generalization== to inference-time inputs longer than those seen in the few-shot exemplars.

Result

Note that these in-domain evaluations are “toy tasks”.

Small models still fail—the ability to perform abstract manipulations on unseen symbols.

==Hence, chain of thought prompting facilitates length generalization beyond seen chains of thought for language models of suffificient scale.==
